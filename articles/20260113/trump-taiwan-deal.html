川普政府接近與台灣達成貿易協議

Trump Administration Nears Trade Deal With Taiwan

ANA SWANSON, TRIPP MICKLE • 2026-01-13
The Trump administration is completing a trade deal with Taiwan that would reduce tariffs on the island’s exports and commit its largest chipmaker, Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Corporation, to invest significantly more in the United States, according to three people familiar with the matter.
據三位知情人士透露，川普政府即將敲定一項與台灣的貿易協議，該協議將降低台灣出口商品的關稅，並要求台灣最大的晶片製造商台積電大幅增加在美投資。
The agreement, which has been under negotiation for months, is being legally scrubbed and could be announced this month. The deal would reduce the U.S. tariff rate, to 15 percent, for goods from the island, the people said. That rate is in line with imports from Japan and South Korea, Asian allies that struck deals last year.
這項已談判數月的協議正在進行法律審查，有望在本月公布。知情人士稱，該協議將把美國對台灣商品的關稅稅率降至15%。這與去年達成協議的亞洲盟友日本和韓國的進口關稅稅率一致。
As part of the deal, TSMC would also commit to building at least five more semiconductor facilities, or fabs, in Arizona, roughly doubling the number of plants it has in the state, one of the people said. The timeline for the investments was not immediately clear. A spokesman for TSMC declined to comment.
其中一位知情人士表示，作為協議的一部分，台積電還將承諾在亞利桑那州新建至少五座半導體工廠（晶圓廠），使其在該州的工廠數量大致翻一倍。目前尚不清楚這些投資的具體時間表。台積電發言人拒絕置評。
Since announcing tariffs on dozens of trading partners in April, the Trump administration has negotiated to reduce those rates in exchange for promises of investments and deals that fulfill U.S. national security priorities. South Korea and Japan have pledged hundreds of billions of dollars in investments in U.S. shipbuilding, nuclear energy, electronics and critical minerals.
自去年4月宣布對數十個貿易夥伴加徵關稅以來，川普政府一直通過談判來降低這些稅率，以換取投資承諾，以及達成符合美國國家安全優先事項的交易。韓國和日本已承諾在美國的造船、核能、電子以及關鍵礦產領域投資數千億美元。
廣告
With Taiwan, the Trump administration wanted the island to invest in more semiconductor manufacturing in the United States. Taiwan dominates global production of chips that are the brains of computers and the data centers needed for artificial intelligence. But relying on the island appears increasingly risky given Beijing’s assertion that Taiwan belongs to China and should one day be brought under Chinese control. China has done live-fire drills around the island, and officials and executives worry about the possibility of an invasion’s disrupting the global supply chains for electronics, cars and weaponry.
川普政府希望台灣加大對美國半導體製造業的投資。台灣在全球晶片生產中佔據主導地位，這些晶片是計算機和人工智慧數據中心的核心部件。但由於北京堅稱台灣屬於中國、終有一天應被納入中國控制之下，這種對台灣的依賴顯得愈發具有風險。中國已在台灣周邊多次進行實彈演習，官員和企業界人士擔憂，一旦發生入侵，可能會擾亂電子產品、汽車和武器的全球供應鏈。
TSMC, the world’s pre-eminent chipmaker, has completed one plant in Arizona since 2020; is completing a second, which will open in 2028; and has promised to build four more in the coming years. But as part of the U.S.-Taiwanese trade talks, it agreed to add at least five more.
自2020年以來，全球領先的晶片製造商台積電已在亞利桑那州建成一座工廠，第二座正在建設中，計劃於2028年投產；公司此前已經承諾未來幾年再建四座。但作為美台貿易談判的一部分，該公司同意至少再增加五座。
The Office of the United States Trade Representative and the Department of Commerce did not provide comment.
美國貿易代表辦公室和商務部均未對此發表評論。
Trade negotiations with Taiwan have dragged on as the two sides have debated what to do about tariffs. The Trump administration has announced limited trade arrangements with countries in recent months in an effort to rework America’s trading relationships. Mr. Trump briefly introduced “reciprocal” tariffs globally in April, before pausing them and then reintroducing them in August.
與台灣的貿易談判之所以拖延，主要是因為雙方在關稅問題上存在分歧。近幾個月來，川普政府宣布與一些國家達成有限的貿易安排，試圖重塑美國的貿易關係。川普於去年4月短暫在全球範圍內推出所謂的「對等」關稅，隨後暫停，並於8月重新實施。
Since then, importers of Taiwanese products have paid a 20 percent tariff when bringing goods into the United States. But the administration exempted semiconductors and many electronics from those tariffs, saying those sectors would be subject to separate national security tariffs issued under a legal provision known as Section 232.
自那以後，台灣產品進口商在將商品運入美國時需支付20%的關稅。但政府對半導體和許多電子產品給予了關稅豁免，稱這些行業將適用根據一項名為「第232條」的法律條款單獨徵收的國家安全關稅。
The administration has also used the national security statute to apply tariffs to steel, aluminum, cars, copper, lumber and other goods. The Section 232 investigation into semiconductors, which is administered by the Department of Commerce, was expected last year. That has prompted speculation that the Trump administration has not pushed forward on it for fear of upsetting a trade truce with China.
政府還利用這一國家安全條款對鋼鐵、鋁、汽車、銅、木材等商品加徵關稅。由商務部負責的針對半導體的「第232條」調查原本預計在去年完成。這引發了外界猜測，認為川普政府遲遲沒有推進調查是擔心破壞與中國的貿易休戰協議。
廣告
The potential tariffs on chips pose a direct threat to the island and TSMC. Semiconductors account for more than a third of Taiwan’s exports, with the most valuable of those chips coming from TSMC’s network of 20-plus factories.
潛在的晶片關稅對台灣和台積電構成直接威脅。半導體佔台灣出口總額的三分之一以上，其中價值最高的晶片來自台積電遍布20多家工廠的生產網路。
The Taiwanese government had earlier concluded trade discussions with the Office of the United States Trade Representative, but continued to discuss the Section 232 tariffs and TSMC’s domestic investment plans with the Department of Commerce. Those talks only recently concluded, as TSMC bought another parcel of land in Arizona for its expansion.
台灣政府此前已與美國貿易代表辦公室結束貿易磋商，但仍與商務部就「第232條」關稅以及台積電在美國本土的投資計劃繼續談判。隨著台積電在亞利桑那州購置了另一塊土地用於擴張，這些談判直到最近才結束。
Administration officials have said companies that invest domestically would not be subject to Section 232 tariffs, though it is unclear exactly how the arrangement will work.
政府官員表示，在美國進行投資的企業將不會受到「第232條」關稅的影響，不過這一安排的具體運作方式仍不完全明確。
Taiwanese officials declined to comment. On Dec. 26, the Taiwanese trade negotiations office said in a statement to reporters that the two sides had reached broad consensus on a trade agreement that would include the U.S. side lowering reciprocal tariffs on Taiwan and providing preferential treatment under Section 232.
台灣官員拒絕置評。12月26日，台灣行政院經貿談判辦公室向記者發表聲明稱，雙方已就一項貿易協議達成廣泛共識，該協議將包括美國降低對台關稅，並根據「第232條」給予台灣優惠待遇。
Earlier in the year, the two sides had been unable to conclude their trade agreement because the bulk of U.S.-Taiwan trade is in chips and the semiconductor tariffs were still evolving, the office said.
該辦公室表示，雙方未能在去年早些時候達成貿易協議，原因是美台貿易主要集中在晶片領域，而半導體關稅政策仍在不斷變化中。
